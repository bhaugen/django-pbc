Getting started

The system has two main sections: Admin and the weekly Operational tabs. Use Admin for initial setup of master data, and flexible viewing or correcting of operational details. Use the Operational tabs for the regular weekly operations cycle, and an easier way to enter product plans.

Use Admin to create the following master information:

  • Food Network
  • Producers
  • Products

    • Products may have other products for parents. Parent products appear in the Category column in product tables. For example, Hamburger per lb may have a parent Beef, which may have a parent Meat. So you would see Meat, Beef in the Category column for Hamburger per lb.

    Typically parent products would not be sold. In other words, you would not sell plain Meat or Beef, but you would sell Hamburger per lb. The categories are useful to make the product tables more readable, and also they may be used for aggregate reports: for example, how much Meat have we sold so far this year?

  • Customers
  • Processors
  • Distributors

Pre-season (or any time): enter plans for producers

Plans are entered by producer, product and date range. Plans ideally would be entered based on last year's demand, maybe adjusted by a growth factor.

The plans determine which producers and products appear in the Availability forms for a given date. If no plans, the producer will not appear in the selection list. If no product plan, the product will not appear in the form once you have selected a producer.

You can use the Plan tab to enter plans for one date range per product for a producer (for example, for the whole season). If you want many date ranges per product for a producer, use Product Plans in Admin.

Weekly operation cycle

  1. Enter what's available this week

    2. Use the Avail tab to enter what's available from producers. Each availability row shows two quantities: Ready and Received. Ready means ready for pickup. Received means received into warehouse inventory. The Custodian field refers to who has the goods other than the Producer. At this stage, that would be a Processor. Later, that might be the Distributor, and the goods might in the distributor's warehouse and so do not need to be picked up.

    The system keeps track of available quantities by deleting deliveries.

    Expiration: Lots of available product expire (no longer are available) after their expiration date. The expiration date is set automatically when the lot is created, by adding the product's expiration days to the lot's creation date. The default expiration is 6 days. In other words, lots will become unavailable next week. If you want all lots of a product to be available for longer, change the expiration days for that product, and new lots will be available longer (but existing lots will not be changed). If you want a particular lot or lots to be available longer, go into Admin and change the expiration date on the lot (Inventory Item).

  2. Send Fresh List emails to customers

  3. Enter orders

    4. Use the Order tab. The form shows all available lots from all producers, and allows you to enter the quantities of each lot that the current customer wants to order. You can also enter notes about customer special requests.

  4. Send pickup list emails to producers and distributors

  5. Send order emails to distributors and customers

  6. Enter meat processing weight and cost

    7. Use the Avail tab and check the meat checkbox to enter the processing information for a producer. Or use the Admin Inventory items section, select the meat item to change, and enter the processing information at the bottom of the page.

  7. Print invoices

    8. Use the Invoice tab to print invoices for each customer order.

  8. Enter payments from customers

    9. You can enter customer payments through the Order tab, or Orders in Admin.

  9. Compute payments to producers, processors and distributors

    10. The coop pays producers etc. after the customer pays the order. The Producer Payments tab tells how much money is due to which producers, and Payment Update computes and enters the payments that will be paid out this week.
